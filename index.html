Procedure

Four steps. Which is three more than the project got.
  1. Name the project and the year it began. The Department appreciates that this is the hardest step.
  2. Select an official Cause of Abandonment — Seasonal Ambition, Scope Creep, I Met Someone (a Different Hobby) — or state your own. The Department does not judge. The Department files.
  3. Pay four dollars. Your case is reviewed by no one and approved instantly, a level of momentum your project never experienced.
  4. Receive your Certificate of Honorable Abandonment — guilt accrued computed to the day — and a numbered entry in the permanent Public Registry. The matter is closed. You are free to go start something else.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answered in full, which around here is remarkable
Is this certificate legally binding?
No. It is emotionally binding, which is the only jurisdiction your unfinished novel was ever going to be tried in.
What exactly do I get for $4?
A permanent page at your own registry number, a computed record of guilt accrued, and a certificate suitable for printing, framing, or attaching to a group chat. This is, for the record, more than the project ever gave you.
What if someone objects to my abandonment?
Any believer may pay $5 to file a formal Objection — “I believe they will finish it” — which is entered beside your discharge, and both documents are stamped CONTESTED. The Department archives disputes. It has never resolved one.
What happens if I finish the project after abandoning it?
Completion of a discharged project is classified by the Department as a miracle, and miracles carry a $3 filing fee. Your certificate is restamped COMPLETED — CLASSIFICATION: MIRACLE and your registry entry updated. Projects do tend to get finished once nobody expects them to be. The Department cannot endorse psychology it merely profits from.
Can I abandon someone else’s project? My husband has a boat.
You may only discharge burdens you personally carry. For the boat, the Department is finalizing Gift Discharges: you pay, he names the project, and the boat becomes a certificate, which fits better in the garage. The waiting list is already substantial.
Is my abandonment made public?
By default, yes — public entries receive the shareable page and appear in the Registry. You may file privately, in which case your entry appears as a number and the word WITHHELD. The Department finds these entries generate the most speculation, and confirms nothing.
Who reads what I submit?
No one, apart from an automated screening for profanity and personal information, which reads everything and enjoys none of it. Entries naming identifiable private individuals are rejected. Any entry may be flagged from its certificate page; entries flagged by three separate households are withheld from public view automatically until the Registrar gets to them. The Registrar accepts correspondence by reply to any Stripe receipt. The Registry files failures, not accusations.
Do you offer refunds?
Yes — reply to your Stripe receipt within 7 days for a full refund, no questions asked. It will be the only request the Department has ever processed without a form.
Is this whole thing a joke?
The seal is satirical. The closure is real. The Department finds these are not in conflict, and four dollars is a competitive rate for the end of guilt.
Official Disclaimer of the Department of Unfinished Business
The Department of Unfinished Business is not a department of anything. It is not a government agency, a notary, a licensed therapist, or an accredited issuer of honors — which is fitting, because your project was not, in any sense, finished. Certificates of Honorable Abandonment are satirical novelty items sold for entertainment purposes. They confer no legal rights whatsoever — a quality they share with every certificate of their kind; ours simply says so on the front. They discharge no actual obligations and will not hold up in court, at work, or in an argument with your mother. The relief, however, is real, and yours to keep. Public Registry entries are screened automatically for profanity and personal information; the Registrar’s decision is final and delivered without comment. Entries flagged by three separate households are withheld from public view automatically, pending the Registrar’s eventual attention; withheld is not deleted — the number and the record remain. Refunds within 7 days. Void where prohibited; valid wherever things go unfinished, which is everywhere.
